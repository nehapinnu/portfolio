WanderHub: Tackling Over-Tourism with
Sustainable, Human-Centered Connections

A collaborative travel platform that helps travelers connect with locals, surface respectful, off-the-beaten-path experiences, and encourage more conscious tourism.

Timeline

January 2024 — April 2024

University of Michigan

Team

7 teammates

Role

Mobile Application Development, UX/UI Design, User Research, Visual Identity, PM

Tools

Figma, Swift, Python

Context

Modern travel apps prioritize scale and popularity, which often funnels people toward the same crowded spots and misses cultural context. WanderHub asks: what if a travel product centered respectful exchange between locals and visitors—and rewarded discovery beyond the algorithmic “top 10”?

⭐ Design prompt: What if the next travel app didn’t just show you where to go—but helped you understand how to go responsibly?

Research & Insights

Competitive Analysis

We audited leading travel platforms and identified gaps in:

  • Direct community involvement and local context
  • Transparency about tourism impact and etiquette
  • Tools that help travelers discover beyond viral lists

User Interviews & Affinity Mapping

  • Interest in authentic experiences with cultural awareness
  • Need to balance spontaneity with safety & respect
  • Locals seek tools to share vetted tips without overexposure

Personas & Scenarios

John Apple — Adventurous Traveler

Wants spontaneous, authentic discovery without extensive planning.

  • Frustrated by tourist traps and repetitive lists
  • Seeks vetted, respectful recommendations

Clairo Orange — Community-minded Local

Passionate about her city; cautious about over-tourism and cultural missteps.

  • Wants to share tips with context and boundaries
  • Seeks tools that encourage etiquette and care

Design Decisions

Explore — Local memos & hidden gems

People can add personal memos to places, creating context-rich discovery. Lists can be saved, remixed, and followed to uncover lesser-known spots.

Connect — Messaging for locals & travelers

Users can follow and chat with locals to gain firsthand insight and etiquette tips, encouraging meaningful connections and safer exploration.

Learn — Etiquette & cultural context

Lightweight guides and community posts provide norms, do’s/don’ts, and cultural expectations—so travelers can show up respectfully.

Product Management

Core Features (Phase 1)

  • Profiles for locals & travelers
  • Real-time messaging with translation
  • “Favorite Places” lists curated by locals

KPIs

  • Lists created & saved per user
  • Creation completion rate
  • Time spent creating lists

Future Features

  • Cultural etiquette toolkits
  • Tips & review system with context
  • Local expert recognition

Feedback Loops

  • In-app surveys & analytics
  • Continuous user research

Results

  • Improved usability scores in playtesting across personas
  • Stakeholder excitement around ethical positioning
  • Clear roadmap toward responsible launch practices

Takeaways

What I learned

  • Co-design with local communities is foundational.

What I’d do differently

  • Test earlier for visual identity resonance.
  • Prioritize accessibility audits from day one.
