Plan, Discover, and Stick with Workouts

Context

Fitness apps used by college students tend to be either too generic (endless feeds) or too rigid (fixed programs behind a paywall). Through interviews and testing, we set out to design something that helps students plan efficient workouts, discover variety, and stay motivated‚Äîwithout adding friction or cost.

Our Solution: a student-friendly fitness hub with a filterable, crowdsourced workout library, smart Google Calendar scheduling, and opt-in gamification‚Äîso you can build a routine that sticks.

Research & Insights

Interviews ‚Äî Identify Needs & Breakpoints

We spoke with U-M students across gender, year, and workout habits. Two consistent needs emerged:

  • Variety & guidance: students do multiple workout types and want fresh, quality ideas.
  • Efficient planning at low cost: they already juggle Notes, Strong, and Google Calendar.

And two big breakpoints:

  • Motivation fades when routines aren‚Äôt visible or progress feels slow.
  • Planning is hard without a clear schedule and quick access to the right workout.

Storyboarding & Speed Dating

We tested 8 storyboard concepts around social, gamification, and personalization.

  • Strong support for Calendar integration to protect workout time during busy weeks.
  • Interest in social features‚Äîbut not a one-size-fits-all leaderboard.
  • Desire for a highly customizable app (length, type, intensity, timing).

Key Design Decisions

Initial Prototype Learnings

Early usability tests (think-aloud) surfaced gulfs of execution/evaluation:

  • Calendar cues: users couldn‚Äôt tell which days had scheduled workouts.
  • Completion feedback: finishing actions lacked visible confirmation.
  • Search & ‚Äúlikes‚Äù clarity: search was overlooked; dumbbell icon read as ‚Äúreps,‚Äù not likes.

Targeted Improvements

  • Added day indicators (muscle-group icons) and a clearer SYNC control for calendar.
  • Introduced post-action overlays to confirm task completion.
  • Made the search bar prominent and clarified the social ‚Äúlike‚Äù affordance.

Subsequent tests showed smoother navigation, faster discovery, and better comprehension.

Final Concept

Based on evidence from two research rounds, our final design incorperates:

  1. New page that shows users all workouts they have created (Gulf of Evaluation)
  2. Calendar clarity to make it clear to users on how to use the calender (Gulf of Evaluation)
  3. Like button was previously represented by a weight icon. Users did not immediately understand
  4. New frame on calender page to make it clear to users that confirms a workout was scheduled for a future date

Together, these features address variety, planning efficiency, and motivation.
